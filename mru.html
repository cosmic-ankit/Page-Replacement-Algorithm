Most Recently Used

(MRU)

A brief introduction to the topic of MRU

The most recently used (MRU) page-replacement algorithm is based on the argument that the page with the smallest count was probably just brought in and has yet to be used.
Hence, the most frequent page, i.e. the page with the highest count will be replaced.

Advantages
  • MRU works well if you have a small number of items that are referenced very frequently, and a large number of items that are referenced infrequently

    • Disadvantages
  • But this algorithm is not commonly used, owing to the fact that on top of the implementation being expensive, it also does not approximate optimal replacement very well.
